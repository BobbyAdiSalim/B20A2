Online Resources

Course Website

Course information, lecture notes, tutorial material, important announcements, etc. will be posted on the course website. It is your responsibility to visit it frequently.

Quercus Page

Course information, lecture notes, tutorial material, important announcements, and grades will also be posted on the course quercus page. It is your responsibility to visit it frequently.

Discussion Board

You are encouraged to use the bulletin board to discuss the course material, pose questions on the assignments, etc. The bulletin board will be monitored by the instructor and by your TAs. Please, be very careful not to reveal (parts of) your solution on the bulletin board.

Email Communication

Questions regarding the course material, assignments, midterm, etc. should be posed on the bulletin board. Questions about the assignment which you feel you cannot formulate without revealing your solution (should be very rare!), as well as questions and concerns regarding your personal matters should be directed to the instructor.

Resources

Find the best research resources for your topic, your course readings and other physical materials your instructor has put on course reserves or short term loan in the library, and the 3 most recent years of exams supplied by the Faculty of Arts and Science (St. George), UTM, and UTSC.

Software and Documentation

  • Relational Algebra Calculator: The relational algebra calculator helps you learn relational algebra (RelAlg) by executing it.
  • SQLite: SQLite is a relational database management system contained in a C library. We will use SQLite for this class.
  • MySQL: An introduction and beginner Tutorial for MySQL.
